Site Purpose

Valoura is a digital job search and employment platform designed to bridge the gap between talented job seekers and forward-thinking employers. The platform empowers candidates to discover and apply for opportunities that match their skills and career goals, while providing employers with efficient tools to attract and evaluate qualified applicants.

Users will be able to:

  • Create and manage personal accounts
  • Browse and search job listings by role, location, or industry
  • Upload and manage resumes and profile documents
  • View detailed job descriptions and company profiles
  • Apply for positions directly through the platform
  • Receive application status notifications
  • Post job openings (employer accounts)
  • Review and shortlist candidate applications

Audience Scenarios

The following scenarios describe how different users might interact with the Valoura platform to meet their goals.

01

How can I find entry-level software development jobs?

Scenario: Maria is a recent computer science graduate searching for her first professional role. She visits Valoura, creates a free account, and uses the search bar to filter listings by "Entry-Level" and "Software Development." The platform surfaces relevant openings sorted by match score and location. Maria reads each job description, views company profiles for culture fit, and bookmarks her favorites. When ready, she uploads her resume and submits her application directly through the platform — receiving an email confirmation and a status tracker in her dashboard.

02

How do I upload my resume and apply for jobs online?

Scenario: James is a mid-career professional returning to the job market after a career break. He logs into Valoura and navigates to his Profile page, where he finds the "Documents" section. He uploads a PDF version of his updated resume and adds a short professional summary. When browsing a project manager listing he finds appealing, James clicks "Quick Apply," selects his uploaded resume from the dropdown, writes a brief cover note, and submits. His application appears instantly in the "My Applications" tab with a "Pending Review" status.

03

How do employers post jobs and review applicants?

Scenario: Priya manages recruiting for a growing fintech startup. She registers an employer account on Valoura, completes her company profile with a logo and description, and clicks "Post a Job." She fills in the role title, responsibilities, requirements, and compensation range, then publishes the listing. As applications arrive, her dashboard displays candidate summaries with resume previews and match-score badges, allowing her to shortlist, message, or decline applicants — all within a single workspace.

Color Schema

Valoura's color palette conveys trust, professionalism, and energy — essential qualities for a platform where career decisions are made.

Navy Blue — Primary

#0A4E8A

Used for the site header, top navigation bar, primary action buttons, section borders, and the page footer. Communicates authority and trust.

Gold — Accent

#F4B400

Used for call-to-action highlights (e.g., "Apply Now" badges), decorative accents, scenario numbers, icon backgrounds, and divider elements. Adds warmth and urgency.

White — Background

#FFFFFF

Used for card backgrounds, content areas, form fields, and modal windows. Ensures readability and a clean, uncluttered interface.

Off-White — Page Background

#F7F9FC

Used as the overall page background to add subtle depth behind white cards and to reduce eye strain during long browsing sessions.

Typography

Valoura uses a deliberate two-font system that balances distinctive personality in headings with clarity and broad compatibility in body text.

Heading Font
Poppins

Applied to all H1–H6 headings, navigation links, section titles, badges, and labels. Weights used: 400 (light labels), 600 (subheadings), 700 (section titles), and 800 (hero headline). Poppins' geometric, friendly letterforms reinforce Valoura's modern and approachable brand identity.

Body Font
Arial

Applied to all body paragraphs, job descriptions, form labels, input fields, and small helper text. Arial is a universally available, highly legible sans-serif that ensures accessibility across all devices and operating systems without additional font loading.

Base Font Size

16 px (1 rem) for body text. Scales up to 18 px on wider viewports.

Line Height

1.7 for body text; 1.25 for headings to ensure tight, impactful display typography.

Accessibility

All text meets WCAG 2.1 AA contrast ratios against their respective backgrounds.

Responsive Scaling

Headings use clamp() for fluid sizing between mobile and desktop breakpoints.
